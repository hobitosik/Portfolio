PRIVACY POLICY

Last Edited on 2015-06-21

compañeros.club LLC (“compañeros.club”, “we”, and “us”) respects the privacy of its users (“you”) and has developed this Privacy Policy to demonstrate its commitment to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy has been compiled to better serve those who are concerned with how their 'Personally identifiable information' (PII) is being used online. PII, as used in US privacy law and information security, is information that can be used on its own or with other information to identify, contact, or locate a single person, or to identify an individual in context. Please read our Privacy Policy carefully to get a clear understanding of how we collect, use, protect or otherwise handle your Personally Identifiable Information in accordance with our website or application.

Please read this Privacy Policy carefully before / while using our application or services or transacting business with us. By using our website or application (our “Service”), you are accepting the practices described in this Privacy Policy.

In case of any questions about our privacy practices, please contact us via contact information provided at the end of this Privacy Policy.

We collect information that can identify you such as your name and surname, and e-mail ("personal information"), as well as other information that does not identify your personality. We collect this above mentioned information via our website and / or a mobile application.

By using the Service, you are authorizing us to gather, analyse and retain data gained through the Service.

Information you provide

You will be asked to sign up using your Facebook login or your personal sign up information, namely, your name, surname, email, age, and gender. If you do so, you agree with our Privacy Policy and authorize us to access certain Facebook account related information, such as your public Facebook profile (consistent with your privacy settings in Facebook), your email address, gender, birthday. You will also be asked to allow compañeros.club to collect your location information from your device when you download or use the Service, if you select to navigate your search with help of GPS. In addition, we may collect and store any personal information you provide while using our Service or in some other manner. This may include identifying information, such as your name, address, email address and telephone number, and, if you transact business with us, financial information. You may also provide us photos, a personal description and information about your gender and preferences for recommendations, such as search distance, age range and gender. If you communicate with other users through our Service, you provide us the content of your chats, and if you contact us with a customer service or other inquiry, you provide us with the content of that communication.

We use various technologies to collect information from your device and about your activities on our Service.

Information collected automatically

We automatically collect information from your browser or device when you use our Service, including IP address, device ID and type, the operating system used by your device, access times, browser type, language, your GPS.

Cookies can be used to facilitate access to our Service and to personalize your experience. We or third parties are allowed to use certain data collected on our Service to show you ads on other web or mobile resources.

Other Technologies

Standard Internet technology. Use of web beacons or similar technologies may be used in our Service, communication (email etc.) and advertisements.

Our service providers may be entitled to display ads, through usage of tracking technologies, such as cookies or web beacons, to collect certain information about user activities related to ads. We don’t provide any non-anonymized personal information to the third parties.

6. CHILDREN’S PRIVACY

We do not allow users younger than 18 years old to use our Services. However, we cannot always control the breach of our Policy.

COPPA (Children Online Privacy Protection Act)

When it comes to the collection of personal information from children under 13, the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) puts parents in control. The Federal Trade Commission, the nation's consumer protection agency, enforces the COPPA Rule, which spells out what operators of websites and online services must do to protect children's privacy and safety online.

We do not specifically market to children under 13.
